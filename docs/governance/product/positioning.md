# Product Positioning - UMS

## Position Statement

UMS is an **Authorization and governance control plane for multi-tenant SaaS**.

## What That Means

UMS helps software vendors and platform teams control who can do what, in which tenant, on which branch, under which business policy, with clear auditability and explainability.

## Target Customers

- SaaS vendors with multi-tenant B2B products.
- Platform teams that need centralized authorization and delegated admin control.
- Security and compliance teams that need explainable access governance.

## Core Problem UMS Solves

Most identity products optimize authentication first. UMS optimizes authorization and governance first, while still supporting federated identity and optional native credentials.

## Why UMS Is Different

1. It is built around a permission graph, not just a user directory.
2. It treats tenant, branch, system, role, and package as first-class business concepts.
3. It is designed for explainability, so support and audit teams can understand access decisions.
4. It is multi-tenant by design, not as an afterthought.
5. It can operate standalone or delegate identity to external providers.

## Messaging Pillars

- Control plane: one place to govern access across a SaaS ecosystem.
- Explainable access: every decision can be traced and understood.
- Tenant-safe delegation: local admins get only the scope they need.
- Business-semantic governance: packages and policies match business operations.
- Extensible by design: external IdPs and integrations remain optional.

## Short Pitch

UMS gives SaaS teams a governed, explainable, tenant-safe authorization layer that centralizes access decisions without locking them into one identity vendor.

## Longer Pitch

UMS is the control plane for authorization and governance in multi-tenant SaaS. It combines tenant-aware delegation, permission graphs, approval workflows, and explainable access packages so product teams can govern access with the same clarity they govern their own business domains. UMS works standalone or alongside external identity providers, which makes it ideal for organizations that want strong control without vendor lock-in.
